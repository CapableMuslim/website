# Strength Training Essentials: Building Power and Resilience

## The Foundation of Strength

**Strength training** builds muscle, increases bone density, improves metabolic health, and enhances overall quality of life. It's not just about aesthetics—it's about functional capacity and longevity.

## Progressive Overload

The key to continuous improvement is progressive overload: gradually increasing weight, reps, or intensity over time. This principle drives adaptation and growth.

## Core Movement Patterns

Master the fundamental patterns: squat, hinge, push, pull, carry. These movements form the foundation of all human movement and athletic performance.

## Actionable Steps

1. Start with compound movements (squat, deadlift, bench press, pull-ups)
2. Follow a structured program with progressive overload
3. Prioritize proper form over heavy weight
4. Allow adequate recovery between training sessions
5. Track your lifts to monitor progress and adjust programming
